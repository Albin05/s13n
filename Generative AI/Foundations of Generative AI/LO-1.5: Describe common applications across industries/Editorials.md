### **1. Title**
Synthetic Fraud Data

### **2. Problem Description**
How does synthetic data help fraud detection?

### **3. Objective**
Understand privacy-preserving training.

### **4. Hint**
Real data is private; synthetic data is safe.

### **5. Short Explanation**
It allows banks to train fraud detection models on realistic data without exposing sensitive customer information.

### **6. Detailed Explanation**
*   **Problem**: You can't share real credit card histories because of privacy laws.
*   **Solution**: Train a GenAI model on real data to learn the *patterns* of fraud.
*   **Application**: Generate millions of "fake" transactions that follow those patterns. Train the fraud detector on this safe dataset.

# **Q13. Implementation – Synthetic Data**

### **1. Title**
Privacy-Preserving AI

### **2. Problem Description**
Generate synthetic fraud data.

### **3. Objective**
Understand how to use GenAI for privacy.

### **4. Hint**
Learn the distribution, then sample.

### **5. Short Explanation**
Train a generative model on real data to learn the probability distribution of fraud. Then sample new "fake" transactions from this distribution.

### **6. Detailed Explanation**
1.  **Input**: Real credit card logs (Private).
2.  **Training**: Model learns patterns (e.g., "Fraud often happens at 3 AM with large amounts").
3.  **Generation**: Model creates new logs that follow these patterns but don't correspond to any real person.
4.  **Result**: A dataset that is statistically identical to the real one but completely anonymous.

### **7. Constraints**
None.
